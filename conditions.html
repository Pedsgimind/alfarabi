Conditions we commonly see

The list below summarises many of the digestive, liver, pancreatic, and nutrition problems evaluated at Al Farabi Centre. It is not exhaustive. If you or your child have symptoms that may be related to the digestive system, please contact the clinic for triage.

Esophagus & stomach

Upper gastrointestinal symptoms in adults and children.

  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) and heartburn.
  • Difficulty swallowing, food sticking, or chest pain.
  • Peptic ulcer disease and H. pylori infection.
  • Unexplained vomiting, early satiety, or weight loss.

Small bowel & colon

Chronic abdominal symptoms and bowel habit changes.

  • Chronic abdominal pain and bloating.
  • Constipation, diarrhoea, or alternating bowel habits.
  • Inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn’s disease and ulcerative colitis).
  • Irritable bowel syndrome and functional bowel disorders.

Liver, pancreas & biliary tree

Evaluation of abnormal liver tests and hepatobiliary symptoms.

  • Fatty liver disease and metabolic-associated steatotic liver disease.
  • Viral hepatitis and autoimmune liver disease.
  • Jaundice, cholestasis, and biliary colic.
  • Pancreatitis and suspected pancreatic disorders.

Nutrition & growth

Feeding, weight, and micronutrient issues in children and adults.

  • Celiac disease, malabsorption, and chronic diarrhoea.
  • Feeding difficulties, oral aversion, and selective eating.
  • Under-nutrition, faltering growth, and obesity.
  • Vitamin and mineral deficiencies related to GI disease.

Bleeding, anaemia & screening

Endoscopic and non-invasive evaluation.

  • Iron deficiency anaemia of unclear cause.
  • Positive stool blood tests.
  • Black, red, or maroon stools.
  • Screening and surveillance for colorectal polyps and cancer.

Other symptoms

Multi-system or non-specific complaints where GI evaluation is helpful.

  • Recurrent nausea, vomiting, or poor appetite.
  • Unexplained weight loss or failure to thrive.
  • Systemic diseases with GI manifestations.
  • Abnormal imaging or lab results requiring specialist review.

This information is for general guidance only and is not a substitute for a medical assessment. Emergency symptoms such as severe pain, vomiting blood, black stools, or signs of shock should be assessed urgently in the nearest emergency department.
